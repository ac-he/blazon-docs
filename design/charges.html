Blazon uses charges to represent integer values.

While more values can be reached using arithmetic operations, the charges listed here act as built-in values to use as a starting point. They can also be printed out for their character value. In addition to charges that represent useful integer values, there are charges that have integer values cooresponding to important characters. There is even a way to specify an 8-bit integer in binary, but this is discouraged as it takes away the fun and won't look as nice.

Some charges require you to specify a tincture while others will auto-select a tincture. Any tinctures you specify must comply with the rule of tincture! The charge tincture will not impact the value.

If a charge supports quantities 1-3, this means that up to three of them may be included. Including two will square the value and including three will cube the value. See the usage column of the chart below for how to specify a plural for each charge.
