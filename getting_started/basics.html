Blazon is the formal language that defines a coat of arms. Blazons are sets of instructions that create a specific images -- and in the case of this language, define a computer program as well!

There is lots of complicated vocabularity involved in heraldry, but for the Blazon Programming Language there are only a few terms to know when you're getting started.

Field

The background of the coat of arms or one of its divisions (subsections).

Charge

A graphic element that occupies a field. The Blazon Programming Language supports a limited set of charges, which are listed here.

Tincture

The color of a specific charge or field. Heraldy traditionally includes seven tinctures, which are listed here.

Dexter

The left of the coat of arms.

Sinister

The right of the coat of arms.

Chief

The top of the coat of arms.

Base

The bottom of the coat of arms.
